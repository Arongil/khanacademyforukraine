You Can Help a Child in Ukraine Learn

For more than 800 days Ukraine has been at war, leaving 2 in 5 kids not able to go to school.

What if we flip the story? What if instead of falling behind, kids get ahead?

Meet Nazar Drugov, a Ukrainian high school student who defied the odds. He studied using free lessons on Khan Academy during blackouts and in shelters during bombardments, and he got into Tufts University.

Just one problem: Khan Academy is in English, and only 1 in 100 Ukrainians knows English.

Our Mission

We're on a mission to translate and localize Khan Academy content into Ukrainian.

  • We've already translated arithmetic, algebra basics, geometry, algebra II, and precalculus.
  • We're currently working on chemistry, economics, and high school statistics.
  • We're collaborating with schools to reach every student in Ukraine.

Let's make STEM education accessible to all 4 million students in Ukraine.
